2. VLAN Concept
What is VLAN?

VLAN = Virtual Local Area Network.

A VLAN separates devices into independent Layer 2 broadcast domains.

Even if devices are connected to the same physical switch, different VLANs behave like separate switches.

10. Why Different VLANs Cannot Communicate

Without a router or Layer 3 switch:

VLANs are isolated
No inter-VLAN routing exists

12. Self-Check Answers
1. Which command creates a VLAN?
vlan 10
2. Which command shows VLANs?
show vlan brief
3. Which command assigns a port to VLAN?
switchport access vlan 10
4. Why can PC1 reach PC2 but not PC3?

Because:

PC1 and PC2 are in VLAN 10
PC3 is in VLAN 20
Inter-VLAN routing does not exist yet
5. What happens if PC3 is connected to VLAN 10 port but keeps IP 192.168.20.10?

Important logic:

Physically PC3 will be inside VLAN 10
But IP address still belongs to subnet 192.168.20.0/24
Devices in VLAN 10 use subnet 192.168.10.0/24

Result:

Communication will fail
Wrong IP subnet for that VLAN
Layer 2 and Layer 3 logic do not match
13. Final Leader-Level Summary
VLAN

Logical Layer 2 separation.

Access Port

Port that belongs to one VLAN only.

Same VLAN

Devices can communicate directly.

Different VLANs

Need routing:

Router
or
Layer 3 switch
Key Understanding

Switches forward frames based on VLAN membership.
IP subnet design must match VLAN design.
